IP PBXs & Gateways

IP (Internet Protocol) is a network protocol that is used to transmit data over the internet or a local area network (LAN). An IP PBX (Internet Protocol Private Branch Exchange) is a type of PBX that uses IP technology to transmit voice and data over the internet or LAN. In an IP PBX system, a gateway is typically used to connect the IP PBX to external networks, such as the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN) or cellular networks. The gateway converts analog or digital signals to IP signals and vice versa, allowing calls to be transmitted over the internet or LAN. There are different types of gateways that can be used with an IP PBX system, including analog gateways, digital gateways, and Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) gateways. Analog gateways are used to connect analog phone lines to an IP PBX system, while digital gateways are used to connect digital phone lines. SIP gateways are used to connect IP-based networks to the PSTN or other communication networks. Overall, IP technology and gateways are important components of IP PBX systems, enabling communication between different networks and facilitating the flow of information. They are also used in other types of communication systems, such as VoIP (Voice over IP) systems, to transmit voice and data over the internet or LAN
